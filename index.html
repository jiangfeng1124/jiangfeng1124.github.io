About Me

I am currently a Senior Applied Scientist at AWS Agentic AI. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral researcher at MIT CSAIL, working with Regina Barzilay. I earned my Ph.D. from the SCIR lab at Harbin Institute of Technology, under the supervision of Haifeng Wang and Wanxiang Che, and was a visiting student at Johns Hopkins University, advised by David Yarowsky.

My research interests span a broad spectrum of topics in natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning. My earlier work focused on low-resource learning, with applications in cross-lingual, task, and domain transfer, as well as AI for chemical sciences. More recently, my work has centered on large language models (LLMs) and agentic AI, including Post-Training (Amazon Titan LLMs), Semantic Parsing (Amazon Q in QuickSight), Information Retrieval / RAG (Amazon Bedrock Knowledge Base), and Agent Primitives (Amazon Bedrock AgentCore).
