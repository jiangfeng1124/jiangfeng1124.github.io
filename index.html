Jiang Guo (郭 江)

I am currently an applied scientist at AWS AI Lab. I obtained Ph.D. degree from the Language Analysis group of SCIR lab at Harbin Institute of Technology, under the supervision of Dr. Haifeng Wang and Prof. Wanxiang Che. From 2014 to 2015, I was a joint Ph.D. student at CLSP in Johns Hopkins University supervised by Prof. David Yarowsky.

Prior to joining AWS, I was a postdoc researcher at MIT CSAIL working with Prof. Regina Barzilay. I was a member of the Natural Language Processing group, and also part of the MLPDS Consortium.

My research interests span a wide range of areas in natural language processing and machine learning. My past research has focused on low-resource learning with applications in cross-lingual, task, and domain transfer, as well as interdisciplinary applications in chemical sciences. Recently I have been mostly working on various topics and applications of large language models (LLMs).
